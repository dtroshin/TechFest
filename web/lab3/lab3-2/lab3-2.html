3.2 - An Applet With Layout

1) The BorderLayout class is located in the java.awt package


			  "A border layout lays out a container, arranging and resizing its components to fit in five

			  regions: north, south, east, west, and center. Each region may contain no more than one 

			  component, and is identified by a corresponding constant"
to initiate we say
Panel p = new Panel();
p.setLayout(new BorderLayout());
p.add(new Button("Okay"), BorderLayout.SOUTH);

region constants include:
  • NORTH
  • SOUTH
  • EAST
  • WEST
  • MIDDLE
  • more info on border layout

2) The GridLayout provides a rectangular grid. 


	"The container is divided into equal-sized rectangles, and one component is placed in 

	each rectangle. For example, the following is an applet that lays out six buttons 

	into three rows and two columns:"
